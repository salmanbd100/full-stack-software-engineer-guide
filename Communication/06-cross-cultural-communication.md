# Cross-Cultural Communication

Adapt your communication style for international technical interviews and remote work.

## Regional Styles at a Glance

| Style | American | European | Asian |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Directness** | Very direct | Balanced | Indirect |
| **Self-presentation** | "I led..." | "Our team..." | "Thanks to my team..." |
| **Disagreement** | "I respectfully disagree because..." | "Perhaps we could consider..." | Rarely direct — ask questions instead |
| **Formality** | Casual, first names | Formal initially | Respect hierarchy |
| **Pace** | Fast, get to the point | Measured | Thoughtful |

---

## American Companies (FAANG, startups)

**What they value:** Ownership, directness, data-driven decisions, enthusiasm.

**✅ Do:**
- Use "I" to show personal ownership: "I led the migration..."
- Be direct about your achievements with numbers
- Disagree with data: "The data shows a 40% improvement, which suggests..."
- Ask clarifying questions without hesitation

**❌ Avoid:**
- Over-modesty: "I just helped with a small part of..."
- Vague answers: give specifics and metrics
- Waiting to be invited to speak

**Common phrases:**
- "Let me dive into this..."
- "The key takeaway is..."
- "At the end of the day..."

---

## European Companies (Spotify, Booking.com, SAP)

**What they value:** Collaboration, process, long-term thinking, documentation.

**✅ Do:**
- Acknowledge team contributions alongside personal ones
- Discuss methodology: "I followed a structured approach to ensure quality..."
- Show long-term thinking: "This solution is maintainable for future teams..."
- Be diplomatic when disagreeing

**❌ Avoid:**
- Aggressive self-promotion
- Rushing the conversation
- Ignoring work-life balance topics

**Common phrases:**
- "Building on what you said..."
- "From my perspective..."
- "Our team collaborated to achieve..."

---

## Asian Companies (Grab, Shopee, ByteDance)

**What they value:** Team harmony, humility, long-term commitment, respect for hierarchy.

**✅ Do:**
- Credit your team: "Thanks to my team's support, we delivered..."
- Show respect for experience: "I learned from senior engineers that..."
- Emphasize long-term thinking and stability

**❌ Avoid:**
- Interrupting or talking over interviewers
- Criticizing previous employers
- Appearing impatient

## Email Etiquette by Culture

**American style (casual, direct):**
```
Subject: Question about the Frontend Role

Hi Sarah,

Thanks for the interview! Quick question — what's the timeline for
next steps?

Best,
Salman
```

**European style (formal, structured):**
```
Subject: Follow-up: Senior Frontend Engineer Interview

Dear Ms. Johnson,

Thank you for taking the time to interview me on [date]. I remain very
interested in the role and would appreciate an update on the timeline
for next steps.

Kind regards,
Salman Rahman
```

> **Rule:** When in doubt, be more formal. You can relax once you know the team's norms.

## Video Interview Etiquette (Universal)

**✅ Always:**
- Test audio/video 30 min before
- Use professional background (plain wall or neutral virtual bg)
- Face the light source — be clearly visible
- Look at the camera, not the screen
- Arrive 2–3 minutes early

**❌ Avoid:**
- Eating or using phone during the interview
- Distracting or cluttered backgrounds
- Muting yourself and forgetting to unmute

**Virtual lag tip:** In video calls, wait 1–2 seconds after the interviewer
finishes before speaking to avoid talking over each other.

## Handling Language Barriers

### When You Don't Understand

```
✅ "I'm sorry, could you please repeat that? I want to make sure I
   understand correctly."

✅ "Just to clarify — you're asking about [paraphrase]. Is that right?"

✅ "I'm not familiar with that term. Could you explain what you mean
   by [term]?"
```

**Never:** Nod along and guess. Always clarify — it shows confidence, not weakness.

### When You Need Time to Think

```
"That's an interesting question. Let me think for a moment..."
"Give me a second to organize my thoughts..."
"I'd like to approach this systematically..."
```

### Paraphrasing to Confirm

```
Interviewer: "Tell me about migrating a complex React codebase."

You: "Just to make sure I'm addressing the right thing — you'd like to
hear about a time I restructured or upgraded a large existing React app?
Is there a particular aspect you care most about — the technical approach,
managing risk, or team coordination?"
```

## Slack & Chat Communication

**State your full question in one message — don't "ping" then wait:**

**❌ Wrong:**
```
"hey"
[waits]
"do you have a sec?"
[waits]
"I have a question about the PR"
```

**✅ Right:**
```
"Hi @john! Quick question on PR #123 — should the error handler return
null or throw an exception? I'm leaning toward throwing since it's
an unexpected state, but wanted your input before merging."
```

**Response time norms:**
- US companies: Same-day responses expected during work hours
- European companies: Next-day is often acceptable; respect off-hours
- Async-first teams: Document decisions in threads, don't expect instant replies

## Safe Small Talk Topics

**✅ Good:**
- Current projects or tech you're learning
- Remote work setup
- Tech events or conferences
- General weather or time zones

**❌ Avoid:**
- Politics, religion, salary (until offer stage)
- Health, personal relationships
- Controversial current events
